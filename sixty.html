At Last

Etta James

1960

"At Last" is a timeless R&B and soul ballad by Etta James, released in 1960. A powerful voice paired with beautiful orchestral melodies creates a captivating song that has the ability to make a person fall in love with love itself.



California Dreaming

The Mamas & The Papas

1965

'California Dreaming' by The Mamas & The Papas is a timeless classic that evokes a sense of longing and wanderlust. The song's beauty symbolizes the romantic and nostalgic yearning of the 1960s era. And best of all: the harmonies.



Wouldn't It Be Nice

The Beach Boys

1966

'Wouldn't It Be Nice' by The Beach Boys encapsulates the optimism and dreams of the 1960s. It makes you appreate youth and the hopeful spirit. The Beach Boys' harmonious vocals and infectious rhythm make it a beloved classic that evokes nostalgia and joy.



Ain't No Mountain High Enough

Marvin Gaye & Tammi Terrell

1967

"Ain't No Mountain High Enough" by Marvin Gaye, released in 1967, is a soulful anthem of enduring love. Gaye's emotive vocals, accompanied by Tammi Terrell's harmonies, convey the message that love can conquer all obstacles. My favorite 60's love song.



Brown Eyed Girl

Van Morrison

1967

"Brown Eyed Girl" by Van Morrison, released in 1967, offers a different take from traditional '60s love songs. With its upbeat rhythm and cheerful lyrics, the song celebrates carefree moments and youthful exuberance. You cannot help but sing along..... "Do you remember when we used to sing? Sha-la-la, la-la, la-la, la-la, la-la tee-da Just like that Sha-la-la, la-la, la-la, la-la, la-la tee-da, la-tee-da"



Born to Be Wild

Steppenwolf

1968

"Born to Be Wild" by Steppenwolf, a rock anthem released in 1968, diverges from the romantic themes of the '60s songs mentioned earlier. With its powerful guitar riffs and defiant lyrics, the song became an anthem for a generation seeking adventure and a break from convention. The sense of independence and adventure can't help but just excite one.
