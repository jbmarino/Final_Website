Sandman

Metallica

1991

Metallica's 'Enter Sandman' is a thunderous heavy metal anthem. It makes you want to turn to heavy metal With its haunting intro, blistering guitar riffs, and James Hetfield's signature growl, 'Enter Sandman' is a relentless and unforgettable sonic assault that has left an indelible mark on the world of music.



Smells Like Teen Spirit

Nirvana

1991

Nirvana's 'Smells Like Teen Spirit' is the iconic grunge anthem that defined a generation and reshaped the music landscape in the early '90s. Kurt Cobain's raw and angsty vocals, paired with the song's explosive guitar riffs, captured the disillusionment and apathy of youth culture. Rest in Piece to Kurt Cobain, a complete star.



Buddy Holly

Weezer

1994

Weezer's 'Buddy Holly' is a catchy and infectiously fun alternative rock track that pays homage to the 1950s rock 'n' roll legend. With its catchy melody and Rivers Cuomo's distinctive vocals, the song became a defining moment for the band, combining nostalgia with a modern twist. Reminds me of my childhood when I was in California.



You Oughta Know

Alanis Morissette

1995

Alanis Morissette's 'You Oughta Know' is an unapologetically angry and cathartic alt-rock ballad that turned heads and broke boundaries. Morissette's raw and emotionally charged lyrics, coupled with her powerful vocals, made 'You Oughta Know' an anthem of empowerment for anyone who's been wronged in a relationship. It can make you cry for hours and then you know your better than the other person.



Don't Speak

No Doubt

1996

No Doubt's 'Don't Speak' is a heartfelt and emotionally charged breakup ballad. Just another iconic breakup song. Yet Gwen Stefani's edginess and raw emotion in 'Don't Speak' set it apart from the rest. She transforms the pain of a shattered relationship into an unforgettable musical experience.



...Baby One More Time

Britney Spears

1998

Britney Spears' '...Baby One More Time' was a game-changer that redefined pop music in the '90s. A true genius, she seamlessly combined catchy pop melodies with imaginative choreography, setting a new standard for the genre. The song's infectious hooks and Britney's magnetic presence not only launched her into superstardom but also left an indelible mark on the music industry. '...Baby One More Time' remains an iconic track that represents the epitome of '90s pop culture. You can't hate this song.
